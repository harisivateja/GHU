Problem Context & Stakeholders

Understanding the challenges of decentralized student data at Horizon State University

The Core Problem

Horizon State University (HSU) lacked an Early Warning System for At-Risk Students. Critical student data resided in isolated departmental systems‚ÄîAcademic Affairs, Financial Aid, Housing, and Student Life‚Äîwith no cross-functional integration or holistic student view. This fragmentation created systemic barriers to effective student support and retention.

Data Accessibility Crisis

Advisors required 15-20 minutes per student to manually query multiple systems, significantly delaying the identification of warning signs and coordination of support services. This inefficiency prevented timely interventions during critical periods when students needed support most.

Reactive Interventions

The university responded to manifest problems (e.g., academic probation after failing grades, financial counseling after payment defaults), missing critical opportunities for preventive action. Students fell through the cracks before warning signs were detected.

Inconsistent Risk Assessment

Different departments used varying criteria for risk evaluation, leading to non-standardized methodology for assessing combined risk factors and prioritizing limited intervention resources. This resulted in inefficient allocation of support services.

Key Stakeholders

AA

Academic Advisors

Pain Point: Excessive time spent on manual data retrieval; difficulty tracking intervention outcomes and maintaining comprehensive student records.
Solution Need: Student 360¬∞ Profile with unified data and persistent Advisor Notes for comprehensive documentation.
DC

Department Chairs

Pain Point: Inability to compare program performance (e.g., Engineering vs. Psychology) or justify resource allocation with evidence-based metrics.
Solution Need: Comparative Department Performance charts and Deep Dive analytics for strategic planning.
FA

Financial Aid Officers

Pain Point: Disconnect between academic struggles and financial payment issues, preventing strategic aid targeting.
Solution Need: Integrated Financial Card in 360¬∞ Profile to enable targeted financial interventions.
IL

Institutional Leadership

Pain Point: Need for high-level, real-time Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) for accreditation and strategic planning.
Solution Need: Dashboard View with retention rate and at-risk student count KPIs for institutional oversight.
